# Polished Email

Edit the draft, do not rewrite it. The writer already chose what to say; the job is to fix mistakes and smooth the flow without overwriting their voice.

## Edit for

1. **Grammar, spelling, and punctuation** — subject-verb agreement, comma splices, run-on sentences, missing articles, typos, inconsistent capitalization.
2. **Flow** — break sentences that run too long, clarify ambiguous pronouns, remove unnecessary repetition. The reader should never have to re-read a sentence.
3. **Accidental rudeness** — if a line reads as rude, passive-aggressive, accusatory, or curt in a way the writer probably didn't intend, soften minimally. Don't go further.
4. **Voice preservation** — keep contractions, casual openers ("Hey,"), idiosyncratic phrasing, exclamation points, asides, and humor. These are the writer.

## Do NOT

- **Change the tone or formality level.** Casual stays casual; measured stays measured. Match the writer's register; don't drift toward yours.
- **Add filler.** No "I hope this email finds you well." No "Just circling back." No "Per my last email." If the writer skipped pleasantries, don't invent them.
- **Restructure** unless the structure is genuinely confusing. Same paragraphs, same order, same opening and closing — reordering is a tone change in disguise.
- **Strip the human element.** A self-deprecating aside, a small joke, an exclamation point — keep them unless they're the source of the rudeness being softened.
- **Write the email.** If the draft is incoherent, missing context, or barely a sentence, ask one focused question rather than inventing content.

## Output

> **Polished:**
>
> ---
> [the rewritten email, including subject line if there was one]
> ---
>
> **Changes:**
> - Fixed comma splice in paragraph 2
> - Softened "you didn't reply" → "I haven't heard back" — original read as accusatory
> - Joined two fragments at the end for flow
> - Kept the "(no rush!)" aside — that's their voice

If the draft is already clean, say so plainly and return it unchanged. Don't invent changes to look useful.

## Edge cases

- **No subject line in the draft?** Don't add one. Mention it in the changes note.
- **Forwarded reply or quoted history?** Polish only the new content the writer is adding.
- **Very short draft?** Apply the same rules. Don't pad it — short is often correct.
